Contributors

Below is the list of writers (in alphabetical order) who had contributed to our zines since 2011. All biographies are written during the time of their contribution and may not reflect their current status. 

CHALANI RANWALA is a media and communication student. She takes English Literature as her minor and is passionate about hint fiction. She is also the Education Officer (2012-2013) in the Student Association of the University of Nottingham Malaysia. Her articles include: A Response to the “System of Competing Ignorance”: Can Taking Charge Really Be the Answer?, Alternative Media: Can the Internet Lead the Way towards Media Freedom?, [untitled](1), [untitled](2).

CHI LENG is an undergraduate student in the School of Modern Languages and Cultures. A passionate doodler, she co-founded The Doodle Box Lah which has participated in numerous Arts For Grabs held in the Annexe Gallery. Her articles include: Hello Up There?, Death List, Feb-March 2013.

DOUGLAS TEOH SHANG EN is a writer who comments mainly on the socio-political situation in Malaysia. Currently pursuing his postgraduate degree in Cultural Studies at a foreign university’s Malaysian campus, he is trying to obtain a deeper appreciation of humanity under conditions of oppression and explore ways of moving forward, both in theory and practice. He has published articles on Aliran Online and various local online news portals. His article includes: Mature, Mindful, and Moderate.

F.Z. is a self-confessed procrastinator who does not like to shop and has a degree that usually triggers blank expressions when mentioned - International Relations. She sometimes speaks gibberish mandarin. Her articles include: More Than a Spring Clean, [untitled], Lamp Posts, Flags, and Megaphones.

H. MUHAMMAD merupakan seorang pemuda yang berasal dari Tawau, Sabah. Beliau sedang berusaha keras untuk menamatkan pe-ngajian beliau dalam bidang sosiologi di Institut Kajian Etnik, Universiti Kebangsaan Malaysia. Beliau juga merupakan editor kepada buku Lastik: Lontaran Batu-Batu Kerikil (2016) yang diterbitkan oleh Gerakbudaya. Artikelnya termasuk: Atas Nama “Rakyat”.

JOSEPH TANG is an undergraduate in the School of Modern Languages and Cultures and is undergoing an exchange programme. He is concerned with the political situation in Malaysia and hopes to witness progressive change in the near future. His articles include: A Culture of Incompetence. Political? Definitely., Sport as Identity, Ignorance is Bliss.

KATHLEEN KANG is an undergraduate student from the School of Psychology in the University of Nottingham Malaysia. Her article includes: From Where I’m Standing.

KIRI KANAN adalah seorang yang keliru. Beliau lebih suka memi-lih jalan tengah kerana selalu bingung menentukan arah semasa memandu kereta. Sekali-sekala, beliau akan muncul di sekitar kawasan Semenyih dan tempat tertentu berhampiran Jalan Bukit Bintang di Kuala Lumpur. Artikelnya termasuk: Jiwa Hamba dalam Konteks Hari Ini, Antara Harapan & Kekecewaan.

MOHAMMED ELNAIEM is an undergraduate student in the School of Politics, History, and International Relations. He is interested in postcolonial and critical theories yet is simultaneously suspicious of them. He advocates the protection of refugees and has volunteered for the Malaysian Social Research Institute (MSRI). His article includes: Dance Puppets Dance.

NAZREEN FAZAL is an ardent writer/poet who is concerned about gender inequality, class discrimination in India, Islamic feminism and many more. She contributes her writings in various platforms and owns a blog titled Penguin Peeks. Her articles include: Islamic Culture or Cultural Islam?, Just Stop!, Today I woke up dead.

NUHA HALIM is always questioning and challenging the status quo. She is a curious, and sometimes confusing, writer who philosophises the everyday life. She is also the research assistant in Nottingham’s Watching The Watchdog research project. Her articles include: Racing with the Sun, Conversations between a Father & a Son, Conversation with Death.

RANIA ABRAHAM is an English language and literature student hoping to find answers in a world full of questions. Her main interests include, feeling discombobulated with the negative representations of Islam within Malaysian society, Malay horror movies and understanding the socio-political arena she fights constant battles in. Her article includes: Kebaya or Abaya(?).

SARA GHAZIE is an undergraduate student in the School of Modern Languages and Cultures. Her article includes: Life as an Exchange Student: a Casual Runner’s Perspective.

SOUMIA MEKKI is a student of International Relations. She enjoys reading, writing poetry and is interested in politics. Her article includes: [untitled].

SOUFIA BHAM writes, paints, photographs, and studies politics and international relations. She occasionally travels to fulfill a painful desire to know more and be more. You can find out about the things that she does on www.mentalarmistice.blogspot.com. Her article includes: Fear, Resistance, and the Power of My Voice.

SI JADONG seorang pendiam yang jahat, bodoh, dan sombong. Dia peminat Heri Dono dan berhasrat satu hari nanti dia boleh menjadi salah seekor Garuda yang comel dalam lukisannya. Artikelnya termasuk: Udang & Batu.

THANT SIN is concerned about the politics in Myanmar at this time of political transition. He has consistently contributed to Global Voices. He is also one of the founders of the SMLC Human Rights Group and is a school ambassador representing his country Myanmar. His articles include: International Communications Studies of Students, Questioning Religion, Yayy! We Got Democrazy Now!, We Create Awareness, We Stop Ignorance, Oh Humans.

TEO LEE KEN adalah adalah anggota kumpulan Diskopi. Artikelnya termasuk: Sosialisme Islam, Garisan yang Menitis.

WANG JIABAO is a researcher engaging in trans-disciplinary research on culture. Her primary research interests revolved around theories of heritage preservation and sustainability in regards to Chinese intangible cultural heritage. Other academic interests include Hong Kong zombie films, the problematic of folk culture and its reproduction, and the dynamics between high culture, popular culture, and folk culture.Her article includes: (Il-l)ogical.

XIAO MING WEE writes about local, regional, and international politics. He is also the Chargé D’affaires of Nottingham University Model United Nation Association and the Head of Delegation for NUMUNA Harvard World Model United Nations 2013. His articles include: One Minute of Your Attention, Please?, Religion & Me: MY-Story, Respect Seriously, Why South China Sea, China?, Counting the Cost of Death, Do You Know What Were You Voting For?.

ZIKRI RAHMAN, seorang pustakawan Buku Jalanan yang berdoa tiap-tiap hari agar Rage Against The Machine bergabung semula dan menyimpan hajat mendengar mereka menyanyikan lagu protes dalam Bahasa Melayu pada suatu masa nanti. Artikelnya termasuk: Tentang Tentang, Merdeka, Tuan.
